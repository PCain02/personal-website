The Art of Wigglegrams

Topic: Artistic Practice | Date: March 25, 2025

Back to Blog

Introduction

Wigglegrams are a fascinating way to bring depth and motion to still images. By rapidly alternating between two slightly different perspectives, they create an illusion of three-dimensionality. This technique is not just a gimmick—it can enhance storytelling, evoke emotion, and add a unique touch to visual work.

What Are Wigglegrams?

Wigglegrams are a type of stereoscopic image that simulates depth by shifting between two images captured from slightly different angles. Unlike traditional 3D photography, wigglegrams don’t require special glasses— the motion itself creates the effect.

Why They Matter

In photography and digital art, depth perception is crucial for immersion. Wigglegrams enhance depth in a way that static images cannot, making them valuable in fields like advertising, social media, and fine art. They add an interactive feel to images, grabbing attention in a crowded digital landscape.

Creating Wigglegrams

The process of making a wigglegram involves capturing at least two images from slightly different angles and then alternating them in quick succession. Software like Photoshop, After Effects, or even simple online tools can be used to animate the transition. Proper alignment and consistent framing are key to a smooth effect.

Historical Wigglegrams

Some of the wigglegrams featured here originate from historical stereoscopic collections, including museum archives dedicated to early 3D photography. These images, captured over a century ago, serve as a testament to the enduring fascination with depth and motion in photography. By adapting them into wigglegrams, I aim to give new life to these vintage perspectives, connecting past techniques with modern digital artistry.

Wigglegrams in My Work

Wigglegrams have played a significant role in my artistic practice. I use them to enhance portraits, add movement to still-life photography, and experiment with surreal perspectives. Their ability to create a sense of life and energy makes them a valuable addition to my creative toolkit.
